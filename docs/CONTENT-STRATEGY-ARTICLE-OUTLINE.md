# Content Strategy: Long-Tail Article Outline

**Domain:** https://themeandcolor.com  
**Context:** New domain (~40 days), low authority, minimal indexation. Goal: organic impressions via informational long-tail content.

---

## 1. Keyword intent analysis

| Keyword | Primary intent | Competition | Dominated by | Fit for low-authority site |
|--------|----------------|------------|--------------|----------------------------|
| **how to choose brand colors** | Informational + commercial | High | Brand/design hubs (Canva, HubSpot, 99designs), listicles | Weak — broad, competitive, big brands own SERPs |
| **best color combinations for websites** | Informational + inspiration | High | Design blogs, tool homepages, listicles | Weak — “best” + “websites” = crowded, tool pages rank |
| **how to check color contrast for accessibility** | Informational + transactional | Medium–high | Contrast-checker tools, WebAIM, W3C, a11y guides | Weak — tool homepages and authorities dominate |
| **color palette ideas for small business** | Informational (ideas, guidance) | **Lower** | SMB/marketing blogs, some design posts; few tool homepages | **Strong** — long-tail, niche, informational, not tool-led |

**Conclusion:** “Color palette ideas for small business” (and close variants) has lower competition, clear informational intent, and is not dominated by palette-generator or contrast-checker tool homepages. SERPs favor practical guides and lists, which a new site can target with a single, focused article.

### H2: Why Your Small Business Needs a Defined Color Palette

- One brand look across website, social, and print.

### H2: How Many Colors Should a Small Business Palette Have?

- Typical range: 3–5 (primary, secondary, 1–2 neutrals, optional accent).
- Primary = main brand color (logo, CTAs); secondary = support; neutrals = backgrounds and text.
- **Internal link (1):** When explaining “starting from one main color,” link to **palette generator** (“build from one base color with our palette generator”).

### FAQ section (schema-ready)

*Each item: one clear question (H2 or H3) + one concise answer (2–4 sentences). Written so they can be marked up as FAQPage schema.*

1. **How many colors should be in a small business color palette?**  
   Answer: Most small businesses do well with 3–5 colors: one primary, one secondary, one or two neutrals, and optionally one accent. Keeps branding consistent without feeling cluttered.

2. **What are good color combinations for a small business website?**  
   Answer: Depends on industry and brand. Common choices: navy or deep blue with white and a warm accent (e.g. gold); soft green or teal with white and gray; or warm neutrals (tan, cream) with a single bold accent. Use a palette generator to explore from one base color and check contrast for text and buttons.

3. **How do I make sure my small business colors are accessible?**  
   Answer: Use a contrast checker to test text and button colors against their backgrounds. Aim for at least 4.5:1 contrast for normal body text (WCAG AA). Reserve low-contrast colors for decoration, not body copy or key buttons.

4. **Can I change my small business color palette later?**  
   Answer: Yes, but consistency over time helps recognition. If you change, update website, social, and key print materials together so the brand still feels coherent.

*(Total FAQ: 4 Q&As, ~150–250 words. Enough for FAQPage schema; no padding.)*
