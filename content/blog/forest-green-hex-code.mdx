## Why forest green hex codes differ

Paint, print, and screens do not share one universal “forest.” On the web, hex is the contract. The **forest green hex code** #228B22 is what `color: forestgreen;` resolves to. Product designers often choose darker tokens for contrast—#065F46 is a common “forest” in Tailwind-influenced systems. Document which hex is canonical in your style guide so engineers do not substitute #006400 or hunter green without review.

British teams searching **forest green colour code** should still list hex first, then optional CMYK or Pantone in print appendices. Regional spelling does not change RGB on LCD panels.

## HSL, CMYK, and design tokens

HSL explains why forest feels wooded: moderate saturation, mid-low lightness—not neon, not pastel. CMYK on coated stock often approximates **C76 M12 Y100 K20**, but paper and ink shift results; proof physically for cartons. Store hex as source of truth; derive RGB/HSL/OKLCH programmatically to avoid token drift.

## Forest green vs hunter green vs emerald

- **Forest green** (#228B22) — CSS named, medium-dark foliage green  
- **Hunter green** (#355E3B) — cooler, deeper, common in apparel  
- **Emerald UI greens** (#059669, #10B981) — brighter, digital-native success colors  

Name hex in briefs. Users forgive variation in photography; they do not forgive three different button greens in checkout.

## Forest green in UI and accessibility

Forest works for nav, success toasts, and eco badges when contrast is tested. White on #228B22 may pass for large bold type but fail at 14px body—use #065F46 for text on white instead. Dark mode: darken or desaturate accents 10–15% on #111827 surfaces. Never rely on hue alone for errors—add icons and labels. In charts, pair forest with blue and amber series for color-blind readability.

## Forest green in branding and print

Parks, garden centers, organic food, and outdoor brands use forest greens for growth and stability. Keep secondaries restrained (off-white, charcoal, muted gold). ## Also searched for

**What is the forest green color code?**  
The standard forest green color code is **#228B22**. UK searches for forest green colour code use the same hex.

**What is the RGB value of forest green?**  
**RGB(34, 139, 34)** for #228B22. Deep UI forest uses RGB(6, 95, 70) for #065F46.

**What colors make forest green?**  
In RYB paint mixing, combine **blue and yellow in roughly a 1:2 ratio** (more yellow than blue), then adjust with black or white for depth. On screen, use #228B22 or test in the color mixer.

**Is forest green dark or light?**  
Forest green is **medium-dark**: darker than lime, lighter than hunter or bottle green. #228B22 reads as rich foliage, not a pastel—use lighter table rows for backgrounds. If you need a near-white green wash, start from Honeydew (#F0FFF0) in the shade table rather than tinting #228B22 with opacity alone, which can look gray on some displays.
